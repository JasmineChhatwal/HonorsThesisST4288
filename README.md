## Honors Thesis Project at NUS- ST4288: Causal Inference Techniques in Supply Chain Management



This project aims at understanding the key components of the Supply Chain industry and its relevance to the current global economy. Due to supply chain’s widespread presence and influence, through this project, we have tried to apply causal inference techniques to model essential supply chain variables and infer cause and effect relationships among them. This is of interest since these would influence decisions taken by the supply chain managers throughout the firm and across the supply chain network. Thus, by using methods such as Inverse Probability Weighting, Outcome Regression and Bayesian Belief Networks, we model the variables of interest, particularly Supply Chain Agility and its impact on performance of the firms, and then infer the magnitude and direction of their causal relationships, if any. Such efforts would enable firms to make quantitatively sound decisions in line with their objectives and goals. We finally compare the findings from our models to existing literature on such relationships to validate the results.
